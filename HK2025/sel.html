Sel Roti: The Golden Ring of Nepal

A crispy, sweet, and sacred snack enjoyed in every Nepali festival and celebration.

History of Sel Roti

Sel Roti is a traditional homemade, sweet rice bread from Nepal, often prepared during festivals like Dashain and Tihar. With its golden-brown ring shape, it symbolizes warmth and togetherness in Nepali homes. Made from soaked and ground rice, Sel Roti has deep cultural and religious value, especially in rural communities.

Key Ingredients

  • Rice Flour: Freshly ground soaked rice forms the base.
  • Sugar: Sweetens the batter naturally.
  • Milk or Water: To adjust the consistency.
  • Ghee or Butter: For richness and flavor.
  • Banana & Cardamom: Optional for aroma and softness.

When is Sel Roti Served?

Sel Roti is typically served during:

  • Dashain and Tihar festivals
  • Wedding and puja ceremonies
  • With tea as a morning or evening snack
  • As a gift to relatives and guests during special occasions

Fun Facts

  • Sel Roti is considered both a food and a cultural offering in Nepali Hindu rituals.
  • Many households still prefer to grind rice manually in a traditional stone grinder (janto) for Sel Roti.
  • It takes skill to pour perfect rings of Sel Roti into hot oil without breaking the shape!
