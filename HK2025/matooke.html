Matooke: Ugandan Plantain Delight

A comforting dish made from steamed green bananas, cherished across Uganda.

History of Matooke

Matooke is one of the oldest and most traditional dishes in Uganda, especially among the Baganda people. It is made by peeling and steaming green bananas, which are then mashed and served with a variety of sauces such as groundnut stew, meat, or beans.

Key Ingredients

  • Green Bananas (Plantains): Peeled and steamed until soft.
  • Banana Leaves: Traditionally used for steaming and keeping the dish warm.
  • Groundnut Sauce or Meat Stew: Common toppings.
  • Onions, Tomatoes, Garlic: Used to flavor the sauces served with matooke.

Serving Variations

Matooke can be enjoyed with beef, chicken, beans, or peanut sauce. In rural homes, it is often cooked over firewood and mashed using traditional wooden tools wrapped in banana leaves.

Fun Facts

  • Matooke is considered Uganda’s national dish and is a must-have at traditional ceremonies.
  • The dish is typically eaten with the hands — no fork needed!
  • Matooke is low in fat and a great source of complex carbohydrates and fiber.
