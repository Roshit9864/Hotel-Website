Masala Dosa: South Indian Delight

A crispy crepe with a flavorful potato filling, straight from the south of India.

History of Masala Dosa

Masala Dosa originated in Karnataka, India, and quickly spread throughout the southern states. It's made from fermented rice and urad dal batter, filled with spiced mashed potatoes, and often served with coconut chutney and sambar.

Key Ingredients

  • Dosa Batter: Fermented rice and urad dal mixture.
  • Masala: Potatoes, mustard seeds, turmeric, onions, curry leaves.
  • Sides: Coconut chutney and sambar.
  • Ghee/Oil: Used to roast the dosa till crisp.

Variations

Variants include Cheese Dosa, Mysore Masala Dosa (spicy chutney inside), Paper Dosa (extra thin), and Paneer Dosa.

Fun Facts

  • Masala Dosa was listed as one of the world's top 10 tastiest foods by CNN Go!
  • The batter requires 8+ hours of fermentation before use.
  • Many restaurants serve dosas longer than 2 feet as a specialty.
