Momo: The Dumpling Delight

Explore the flavors, history, and love for Nepal’s favorite comfort food.

History of Momo

Momo, a type of South Asian dumpling, is believed to have originated in Tibet and gained popularity in Nepal, Bhutan, and Northeast India. In Nepal, momo became an iconic street food and household favorite. Originally filled with yak meat, it now comes in various vegetarian and non-vegetarian varieties.

Popular Types of Momo

  • Steam Momo: The classic and most popular style.
  • Fried Momo: Crispy outside, juicy inside.
  • Jhol Momo: Served in a spicy and tangy soup/gravy.
  • Chilli Momo: Stir-fried with onions, capsicum, and sauces.
  • Open Momo: Creative twist with open-faced folds.

Common Ingredients

Momo wrappers are made from simple dough (flour + water). Fillings include minced chicken, buff, pork, paneer, vegetables, ginger, garlic, onion, coriander, and Nepali spices. Served with tomato or sesame-tomato chutney.

Fun Facts

  • Momo is so loved in Nepal that there's a "National Momo Day" celebrated in some areas!
  • There are more than 50 variations of momo across Nepal.
  • “Momo Craze” restaurants have opened in Europe, the US, and Australia due to its global popularity.
