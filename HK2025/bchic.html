Butter Chicken: The Creamy Classic

Discover the rich, velvety delight that defines North Indian comfort food.

History of Butter Chicken

Butter Chicken, or Murgh Makhani, was created in Delhi in the 1950s by Kundan Lal Gujral, a chef who wanted to reuse leftover tandoori chicken by simmering it in a rich tomato-butter-cream sauce. Since then, it has become one of India’s most iconic dishes.

Key Ingredients

  • Chicken: Often marinated in yogurt and spices, then grilled.
  • Tomato Purée: Creates the rich red base.
  • Butter & Cream: Give it its signature velvety texture.
  • Spices: Garam masala, chili powder, ginger, garlic, and fenugreek leaves.
  • Optional: Coriander garnish, paired with naan or basmati rice.

Variations and Pairings

Butter Chicken can be adjusted for richness by using cashew paste or coconut milk as a substitute for cream. It pairs best with butter naan, garlic naan, roti, or plain basmati rice. For a twist, serve it with jeera rice or pulao.

Fun Facts

  • Butter Chicken inspired the famous British dish "Chicken Tikka Masala."
  • It’s among the most ordered Indian dishes in restaurants globally.
  • Some chefs even create Butter Chicken Pizza and Pasta fusions!
